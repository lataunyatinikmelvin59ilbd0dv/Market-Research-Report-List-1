<p><p>Biological fungicides are natural alternatives to chemical fungicides, divided into two main types: microbial and botanical. Microbial fungicides contain beneficial microorganisms that compete with harmful fungi, while botanical fungicides are derived from plants and contain compounds that inhibit fungal growth. Both types offer effective control of fungal diseases while being safe for the environment and beneficial insects. The microbial market is characterized by products containing bacteria or fungi such as Bacillus subtilis or Trichoderma spp, while the botanical market includes products made from plant extracts like neem oil or garlic.</p></p>
