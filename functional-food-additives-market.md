<p><p>Functional food additives are substances added to food to enhance its taste, texture, or shelf life. Sweeteners are additives that provide sweetness without the added calories of sugar. Preservatives help prolong the shelf life of food by preventing spoilage. Emulsifiers are used to blend ingredients together smoothly. Thickening agents are additives that give food a thicker consistency. Other types of functional food additives include antioxidants, colorants, and flavor enhancers, all of which serve various purposes in improving the quality and appeal of food products.</p></p>
