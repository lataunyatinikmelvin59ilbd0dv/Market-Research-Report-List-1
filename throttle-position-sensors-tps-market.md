<p><p>Throttle Position Sensors (TPS) can be categorized into two main types: End Switches Type and Potentiometer Type. End Switches Type TPS are equipped with switches that activate when the throttle reaches a specific position, providing binary feedback. On the other hand, Potentiometer Type TPS utilize a variable resistor to track the exact position of the throttle, providing continuous feedback. These different types cater to varying needs in the automotive industry, offering precise throttle control and feedback.</p></p>
