<p><p>Carbon tool steel is classified into three main types based on its carbon content: 0.60–0.75%, 0.76–0.90%, and 0.91–1.50%. The 0.60–0.75% carbon tool steel is known for its good balance of hardness and toughness, making it suitable for various cutting tools. The 0.76–0.90% carbon steel offers higher hardness and wear resistance, ideal for applications requiring high cutting speeds. On the other hand, the 0.91–1.50% carbon steel provides maximum hardness and wear resistance, often used for heavy-duty cutting tools and dies.</p></p>
