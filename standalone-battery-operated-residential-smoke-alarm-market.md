<p><p>The market for standalone battery-operated residential smoke alarms includes three types: photoelectric smoke alarms, ionization smoke alarms, and combination smoke alarms. Photoelectric smoke alarms use light sensors to detect smoke particles, making them ideal for detecting slow, smoldering fires. Ionization smoke alarms use ionized particles to detect fast, flaming fires. Combination smoke alarms combine both photoelectric and ionization technologies for dual detection capabilities, providing comprehensive protection against various types of fires. These different types cater to diverse customer preferences and safety needs in the residential sector.</p></p>
