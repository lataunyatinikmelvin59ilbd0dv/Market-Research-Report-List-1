<p><p>Wearable drug delivery systems come in two main types: electronic drug delivery devices and mechanical drug delivery devices. Electronic drug delivery devices utilize technology to deliver drugs through processes like iontophoresis or microneedles. These systems are often more precise and customizable. Mechanical drug delivery devices, on the other hand, rely on physical mechanisms to administer drugs, such as patches or pump systems. Both types of systems offer convenient and efficient ways to deliver medication to patients in a wearable format.</p></p>
