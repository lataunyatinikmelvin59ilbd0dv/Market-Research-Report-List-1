<p><p>Plant cellulose fiber is primarily divided into two types, natural and synthetic. Natural cellulose fibers are derived from plants such as cotton, jute, and flax, and are environmentally friendly and biodegradable. They are commonly used in textiles, paper, and cosmetics. On the other hand, synthetic cellulose fibers, like rayon and modal, are chemically processed to imitate the properties of natural fibers. They are often used in clothing and home furnishings due to their softness and durability. Both types of cellulose fibers play a significant role in various industries globally.</p></p>
