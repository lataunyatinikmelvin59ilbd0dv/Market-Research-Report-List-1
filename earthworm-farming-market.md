<p><ul><li>Eudrilus Eugeniae</li><li>Eisenia Fetida</li><li>Aporrectodea Calignosa</li></ul></p>
<p><p>Earthworm farming involves raising specific types of earthworms for various purposes. Eudrilus Eugeniae, also known as African nightcrawlers, are popular for vermicomposting due to their ability to quickly turn organic waste into nutrient-rich compost. Eisenia Fetida, or red wigglers, are commonly used in home vermicomposting systems for their efficiency in breaking down organic matter. Aporrectodea Calignosa, or gray worms, are used in larger-scale agricultural operations for soil improvement and nutrient cycling. Each type of earthworm has unique qualities that make them suitable for different markets within the earthworm farming industry.</p></p>
