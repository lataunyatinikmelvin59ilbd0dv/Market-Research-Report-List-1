<p><p>QC and PD IC Market Types refer to the different roles that a port can have in a USB Type-C connection. Downstream Facing Port (DFP) is the host port that provides power and data to connected devices. Upstream Facing Port (UFP) is the device port that receives power and data from the host. Dual Role Port (DRP) is a port that can switch between DFP and UFP roles depending on the connection. These different port types determine how power and data flow in a USB Type-C system.</p></p>
