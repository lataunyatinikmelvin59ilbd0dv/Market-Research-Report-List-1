<p><p>Laminar composites are materials made by bonding together layers of different materials to create a stronger, more durable product. The market types for laminar composites include brazing, coextrusion, explosive bonding, roll bonding, and others. Brazing involves joining materials using a filler metal, while coextrusion involves combining multiple materials simultaneously. Explosive bonding uses explosives to bond materials together, and roll bonding involves passing materials through rollers to create a strong bond. Other methods for creating laminar composites include diffusion bonding and diffusion-clad bonding.</p></p>
