<p><p>Oleochemical fatty acids are divided into two main types based on their chemical structure: unsaturated fatty acids and saturated fatty acids. Unsaturated fatty acids have at least one double bond in their carbon chain, making them more flexible and liquid at room temperature. Saturated fatty acids, on the other hand, have no double bonds and are usually solid at room temperature. Both types of fatty acids have different applications in industries such as cosmetics, food, and pharmaceuticals due to their unique properties.</p></p>
