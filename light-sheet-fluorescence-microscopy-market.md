<p><p>Light Sheet Fluorescence Microscopy involves three main market types: Single Plane Illumination Microscopy (SPIM), Dual- or Di-SPIM, and other emerging technologies. SPIM uses a single plane of light to image a sample, reducing photodamage and increasing imaging speed. DiSPIM enhances SPIM by using two opposing objectives to capture multiple views of the sample. Other emerging technologies in the market include lattice light sheet microscopy and selective plane illumination with structured illumination. These technologies offer improved resolution, imaging speed, and reduced photodamage for various biological and biomedical applications.</p></p>
