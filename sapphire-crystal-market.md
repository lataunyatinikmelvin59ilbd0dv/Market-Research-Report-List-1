<p><p>Sapphire crystal is divided into two main market types: artificial sapphire crystal and natural sapphire crystal. Artificial sapphire crystals are man-made in industrial settings using a process called Verneuil process, which involves melting aluminum oxide powders. These crystals are used in various applications such as electronic devices, optics, and watchmaking due to their strength and scratch resistance. On the other hand, natural sapphire crystals are mined from the earth and are highly valued for their rarity, beauty, and natural properties. They are primarily used in luxury jewelry and gemstone markets.</p></p>
